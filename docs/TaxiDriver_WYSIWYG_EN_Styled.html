TaxiDriver

Your City. Your Shift. Your Reputation.

A complete taxi-driving mode for BeamNG.drive free roam.

Dynamic requests · Real passenger behavior · Mobile navigation · Fares and ratings

Version 2.10.6 · Designed for BeamNG.drive 0.38.6

Turn free roam into a living taxi experience.

Go online, compare available fares, pick up passengers, follow the route, maintain a strong driver rating, and earn more for safe and comfortable driving.

📱 Not a Scenario — A Complete Free Roam Mode

TaxiDriver runs as a standalone UI App inside BeamNG.drive free roam. Add TaxiDriverHUD to your interface, place the phone wherever it is comfortable, and start or stop your shift whenever you want.

The entire experience lives inside the driver's mobile phone:

  • going online and offline;
  • searching for requests;
  • comparing passengers and fares;
  • navigation and ETA;
  • pickup, boarding, scheduled stops, and drop-off;
  • fare calculation and penalties;
  • driver rating and earnings bonuses;
  • language, difficulty, text size, guidance, and sound settings.

No separate scenario. No fixed map-specific trip list. Just add the phone and go online.

🚕 Choose the Ride That Fits You

After you go online, the phone simulates connecting to dispatch and gradually builds a mixed pool of 10–12 available requests.

Every request card shows:

  • the passenger's randomly generated English name;
  • Passenger Calmness;
  • pickup deadline;
  • ride distance;
  • estimated duration;
  • estimated payout;
  • driver-rating bonus;
  • rush bonus or scheduled-stop count when applicable.

Standard Rides

Balanced city work with a clear pickup, destination, time estimate, and fare.

Rush Requests

Accept a tighter time limit for extra money. If the timer expires, you keep the base fare and lose only the rush bonus.

Rides with Stops

Take a longer route through two intermediate roadside points. Remain stationary for 10 seconds at each stop while the phone displays a dedicated waiting loader.

Sparse map protection: multi-stop requests appear only when the current map provides enough suitable locations. Otherwise, TaxiDriver safely generates regular work instead of stalling the dispatcher.

💬 Passengers Feel Like Passengers

Names and Emoji Messages

Every passenger receives a randomly combined English first and last name.

After accepting a request, the selected passenger may send one to three emoji-only messages while you drive to the pickup point. Each conversation keeps one coherent mood:

  • cheerful;
  • sad;
  • excited;
  • sleepy;
  • dreamy;
  • grumpy.

Messages belong only to the selected passenger and active pickup session. They never appear while browsing the dispatcher and do not require translated sentences.

Passenger Calmness: 0% to 100%

Calmness changes how the same driving event affects the fare:

  • At 100% Calmness: an eligible penalty can have up to a 70% chance of being ignored.
  • At or above 50%: the passenger does not increase the normal penalty value.
  • Below 50%: applied penalties become progressively stronger.
  • At 0% Calmness: penalties are never ignored and can reach twice their normal value.

A Passenger Can End the Ride

Dangerous driving increases passenger dissatisfaction. If it becomes critical, the passenger may demand an immediate stop:

  1. The phone switches to a dedicated emergency screen.
  2. The vehicle brakes automatically.
  3. Driving controls are temporarily locked after the car nearly stops.
  4. The passenger door opens and the passenger exits.
  5. The door closes, controls are restored, and the taxi returns to work.

An early passenger exit pays no fare and reduces the overall driver rating according to difficulty—from 10% on Elementary to 45% on Professional.

🗺️ Realistic Dynamic Locations

TaxiDriver does not store a hard-coded list of coordinates for individual maps. It prioritizes locations that make sense for a taxi:

  • bus stops;
  • street-side and regular parking spaces;
  • points of interest;
  • other compatible semantic map objects;
  • safe road-edge fallback positions.

The selected point is projected toward the outer edge of the road, taking road width and legal direction into account. This reduces pickups in the middle lane of a multi-lane road.

Semantic locations receive controlled variation along the same road segment. Recently generated positions are avoided when alternatives exist, so pickup and destination points feel less repetitive.

On maps with few suitable places, recent positions remain available as a fallback. This keeps order generation reliable instead of sacrificing the entire request pool for variety.

Normal ride distance is limited to approximately 1–25 kilometers.

🚪 Pickup, Boarding, Stops, and Drop-Off

When you stop at the target, the phone displays a dedicated boarding or drop-off screen with a loading indicator.

TaxiDriver then attempts to:

  1. identify a suitable passenger door;
  2. open it;
  3. wait briefly;
  4. close it and continue the ride.

Door triggers differ between vehicles, so this animation works on a best-effort basis. If no compatible trigger exists, the ride continues normally.

Pickup Deadline

Every accepted request starts a calculated pickup countdown. Arriving too late creates a visible fare penalty. The reduction begins at 5% and grows gradually up to a maximum of 12%.

Scheduled Stops

At every intermediate stop, remain stationary for 10 seconds. Moving away or leaving the point resets the waiting timer.

📍 A Real Mobile Navigation Experience

During an active route, the phone displays an adapted rectangular BeamNG.drive minimap. It appears only on navigation screens and remains behind TaxiDriver's localized notifications.

The navigation screen includes:

  • the real route line;
  • estimated arrival time;
  • remaining distance;
  • current speed limit when available;
  • progress toward pickup or destination;
  • intermediate-stop markers positioned across the complete ride progress bar.

Dynamic Zoom

The minimap moves closer at low speed and progressively zooms out as the vehicle accelerates. The transition is smoothed to avoid distracting jumps during city driving.

Optional Road Guidance

Navigation arrows drawn directly over the road can be disabled in settings. The route remains visible on the phone map.

ETA is planned around an average urban speed of 40 km/h. It is an estimate, not a required driving speed.

💵 Your Driving Changes Your Pay

The fare combines a base charge, route distance, and expected travel time. While you drive, TaxiDriver evaluates confirmed events rather than punishing every small fluctuation.

What Can Reduce the Fare?

  • Speeding: uses a tolerance margin and grace period before applying a penalty.
  • Collisions: detects increases in vehicle damage and avoids repeated charges for one impact.
  • Harsh maneuvers: evaluates acceleration, braking, and lateral cornering forces.
  • Late pickup: applies a moderate reduction that grows while the passenger waits.
  • Expired rush timer: removes only the bonus portion of the fare.

Every applied event appears in the phone's dedicated Penalties list. The combined fare reduction is capped at 50%.

A strong reputation pays.

Ratings above 3.99 receive a proportional earnings bonus, reaching +15% at a perfect 5.00 driver rating.

The ride screen displays a five-star progress bar filled according to the precise rating value from 0.00 to 5.00.

🔄 Stay Online Between Rides

After more than 90% of the current route is complete, the phone may display another request. You have five seconds to accept it.

  • An ignored offer expires completely.
  • Another offer may appear after 5–10 seconds.
  • An accepted offer enters a queue and never replaces the current passenger.
  • After drop-off, TaxiDriver automatically directs you to the queued passenger.
  • The green accepted-request confirmation disappears when that passenger begins boarding.

⚙️ Configure the Phone

Open settings from the gear button, save your preferences, and return directly to the previous phone screen.

  • Language: English, Russian, German, French, Spanish, Polish, and Ukrainian.
  • Remember Language: save a language other than the default English.
  • Text Size: increase interface text without resizing the entire phone.
  • Difficulty: Elementary, Easy, Standard, or Professional.
  • Road Guidance: show or hide arrows and markers drawn over the road.
  • Silent Mode: mute every TaxiDriver application sound.
  • Minimize Phone: smoothly slide the phone down when you need a clear road view.

Six Dedicated Sound Groups

Interface clicks, going online, going offline, floating offers, penalties, and passenger messages all follow BeamNG.drive's Interface Volume slider in real time.

Reliable Settings Storage

Preferences are stored in:

BeamNG.drive/current/settings/TaxiDriver/settings.json

Invalid or unsupported settings automatically fall back to safe defaults and are recreated.

🎮 Getting Started

  1. Place taxidriver.zip in BeamNG.drive/current/mods.
  2. Enable the mod in Mod Manager.
  3. Open the UI Apps editor.
  4. Add TaxiDriverHUD.
  5. Press Start Driving.
  6. Wait for requests and choose your first passenger.

Important: do not keep packed and unpacked copies of the same mod version at the same time. Duplicate Lua and UI files may cause loading conflicts.

Compatibility and Limitations

  • Target version: BeamNG.drive 0.38.6.
  • The current map must provide a usable road graph.
  • Semantic location quality depends on map-provided bus stops, parking spaces, and points of interest.
  • Maps with very few suitable places may reuse recent roadside points.
  • Door animation support depends on the selected vehicle's trigger structure.
  • The mod is designed primarily for free roam and is not a standalone scenario.
